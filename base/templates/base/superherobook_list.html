SuperHero Books

"Watchmen" by Alan Moore

Harry discovers he's a wizard on his 11th birthday when Hagrid escorts him to magic-teaching Hogwarts School. As a baby, his mother's love protected him and vanquished the dark lord, leaving Harry with a lightning-bolt scar.

"Batman: Year One" by Frank Miller

A gritty and realistic retelling of Batman’s origin story, focusing on his first year as the Dark Knight and his initial battles against corruption in Gotham City.

"All-Star Superman" by Grant Morrison

A critically acclaimed series that captures the essence of Superman, chronicling his adventures as he faces his mortality and reflects on his legacy.

"Ms. Marvel: No Normal" by G. Willow Wilson

The story of Kamala Khan, a Muslim teenager from Jersey City, who discovers her shape-shifting powers and takes on the mantle of Ms. Marvel.

"Invincible" by Robert Kirkman

A coming-of-age story about Mark Grayson, a teenager who inherits his father's superpowers and learns the ropes of being a superhero, filled with action and emotional depth.

"Kingdom Come" by Mark Waid

In his sixth year, Harry discovers a mysterious potions book belonging to the 'Half-Blood Prince.' As he delves deeper into Voldemort's past, Harry prepares for the ultimate battle.

"The Dark Knight Returns" by Frank Miller

A seminal work that redefines Batman for a modern audience, focusing on an older Bruce Wayne coming out of retirement to combat crime in a dystopian Gotham.

"Spider-Man: Blue" by Jeph Loeb

A poignant story that explores Peter Parker’s love life, particularly his relationship with Gwen Stacy, and how her death affected him.

"Wonder Woman: Blood" by Brian Azzarello

A fresh take on Wonder Woman’s origin, blending mythology with modern storytelling as Diana discovers her true heritage and battles the gods.

"The Umbrella Academy: Apocalypse Suite" by Gerard Way

A quirky and imaginative story about a dysfunctional family of adopted superhero siblings who must reunite to solve the mystery of their father's death and save the world.

"X-Men: God Loves, Man Kills" by Chris Claremont

A powerful narrative that tackles themes of prejudice and intolerance, as the X-Men fight against a radical preacher who views mutants as an abomination

"Black Panther: A Nation Under Our Feet" by Ta-Nehisi Coates

A deep and politically charged story that sees T'Challa, the Black Panther, grappling with internal strife and external threats to Wakanda.
