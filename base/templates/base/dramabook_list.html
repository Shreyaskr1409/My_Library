Drama Books

To Kill a Mockingbird by Harper Lee

Set in the 1930s South, this classic follows Scout Finch as she learns about justice and morality through her father, lawyer Atticus Finch, during a racially charged trial.

Death of a Salesman by Arthur Miller

A poignant play about Willy Loman, an aging salesman grappling with his failed dreams and strained family relationships, exploring themes of ambition and identity.

A Streetcar Named Desire by Tennessee Williams

Blanche DuBois, a fragile Southern belle, clashes with her brutish brother-in-law, Stanley Kowalski, in New Orleans, leading to a tragic downfall. Themes include desire and mental illness.

The Crucible by Arthur Miller

During the Salem witch trials, John Proctor fights against hysteria and paranoia in his Puritan community, examining themes of mass hysteria and integrity.

The Importance of Being Earnest by Oscar Wilde

A witty comedy of manners that satirizes Victorian society, focusing on two friends who create alter egos to escape social obligations. Themes include identity, marriage, and societal expectations.

Long Day's Journey into Night by Eugene O'Neill

A raw depiction of the Tyrone family's dysfunction over a single day, revealing their addictions and regrets. Themes include family dynamics and addiction.

Who's Afraid of Virginia Woolf? by Edward Albee

George and Martha invite a younger couple for a night of drinking that devolves into explosive confrontations, exploring themes of marital strife and illusion versus reality.

The Glass Menagerie by Tennessee Williams

Tom Wingfield narrates the struggles of his family during the Great Depression, focusing on his overbearing mother and fragile sister. Themes include escape and the fragility of dreams.

Hamlet by William Shakespeare

Prince Hamlet seeks revenge for his father's murder in this renowned tragedy, exploring themes of existence, madness, and revenge through profound soliloquies and an intricate plot.

Fences by August Wilson

Troy Maxson, a former baseball player turned sanitation worker, struggles with missed opportunities and family conflicts in the 1950s, highlighting themes of duty and the African American experience.

The Cherry Orchard by Anton Chekhov

A Russian aristocratic family faces the sale of their cherry orchard, symbolizing societal change at the turn of the 20th century. Themes include loss and the clash of old and new orders.

A Raisin in the Sun by Lorraine Hansberry

This powerful play follows the Younger family as they strive to improve their lives amidst racial tensions and economic hardship in 1950s Chicago. Themes include dreams, identity, and racial discrimination.
